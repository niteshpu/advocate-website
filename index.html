About Adv. Nimesh Suthar

With more than two decades of continuous practice, Adv. Nimesh Suthar has appeared in a wide range of matters before the Rajasthan High Court, including constitutional writs, land and revenue disputes, service law, RTI applications and appeals, and both civil and criminal litigation.

The approach is rooted in thorough preparation, clear advice, and ethical practice—ensuring that every brief is handled with the seriousness it deserves, whether for an individual, a public servant, or an institution.

Core Values

  • Integrity and candour with clients
  • Meticulous research and drafting
  • Clarity in strategy and communication
  • Respect for court and due process

Key Strengths

  • Deep understanding of Rajasthan service rules
  • Experience in land & revenue records and disputes
  • Effective use of RTI for transparency
  • Strong written submissions and oral arguments

Practice Areas

Comprehensive legal representation across a wide spectrum of matters before the Rajasthan High Court.

Writ Petitions

Drafting and arguing writ petitions under Articles 226 and 227 challenging administrative and quasi-judicial actions, service orders, and violations of fundamental and legal rights.

RTI Matters

Advising on drafting RTI applications, first and second appeals, and challenging non-disclosure or improper denial of information through writ jurisdiction.

Land & Revenue Cases

Handling disputes relating to land records, mutation, agricultural land, ceiling matters, and revenue entries before revenue authorities and the High Court.

Service & Employment Law

Challenging adverse service orders, transfers, suspensions, disciplinary proceedings, promotions, seniority, and pensionary benefits of government employees and public servants.

Civil Litigation

Civil appeals, second appeals, and miscellaneous petitions arising out of property disputes, contracts, injunctions, and execution proceedings.

Criminal Matters

Bail applications, criminal revisions, quashing petitions, and appeals arising from proceedings before subordinate courts and special courts in Rajasthan.

Professional Journey

A brief overview of more than two decades of legal practice.

2003

Enrollment as Advocate

Enrolled with the Bar Council and commenced practice with a focus on civil and criminal matters in district courts of Rajasthan.

2008

Shift to High Court Practice

Transitioned practice primarily to the Rajasthan High Court, gradually building experience in writ jurisdiction and service law.

2013

Specialisation in Land & Service Matters

Developed a focused practice in land & revenue disputes and service matters of state government employees, including teachers, police personnel, and other departments.

2020

Extensive RTI & Public Law Work

Regularly advising on RTI strategy and filing writ petitions to ensure transparency and accountability in public administration.

Present

Independent Practice

Continuing independent practice before the Rajasthan High Court with a strong emphasis on well-researched written submissions and effective court craft.
